Mission guardrails

Responsible AI & Ethical Guidelines.

At CAILI, responsible AI means using models to widen thinking, document reasoning, and stay accountable to the people affected by the work. These principles keep the studio useful without turning it into a shortcut machine.

Fairness Lens

Check for missing voices, uneven impact, and untested assumptions.

Community Lens

Keep Baltimore communities visible in the problem, not outside it.

CAILI Fairness Transparency Integrity Community

AI should deepen judgment, not replace it.

Question assumptions Document decisions Keep human accountability
Transparency Lens

Show sources, disclose AI support, and make limits clear.

Integrity Lens

Let AI support structure while your own reasoning does the work.

Fairness & Equity

Use AI to widen perspective, test assumptions, and spot who may be overlooked by a decision.

Transparency

Show your sources, disclose AI support when required, and make the limits of generated output visible.

Academic Integrity

Let AI support brainstorming and structure, but keep your own analysis, voice, and conclusions at the center.

Community Engagement

Use AI in ways that strengthen trust, social mobility, and real partnership with Baltimore communities.

Do

Use AI to explore, verify, and refine

  • Use AI to summarize readings, compare cases, and structure outlines.
  • Verify facts, cite sources, and disclose assistance when required.
  • Revise generated material into your own analysis and voice.
Don’t

Outsource judgment, authorship, or accountability

  • Do not submit AI output as final work without review and revision.
  • Do not conceal model use when a course or faculty member requires disclosure.
  • Do not outsource legal, policy, or factual judgment to AI alone.

Review UBalt’s Academic Integrity Policy and AI in the Classroom guidance for classroom expectations.
